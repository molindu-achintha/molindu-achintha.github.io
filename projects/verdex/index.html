Verdex: AI-Powered Crop Disease Identifier

A mobile application leveraging deep learning to provide instant, accurate disease diagnosis and localized treatment recommendations for Sri Lankan rice farmers, bridging the critical knowledge gap in smallholder agriculture.

Executive Summary

Verdex is an AI-powered mobile application developed using Flutter and PyTorch CNN, with a cloud-deployed backend on AWS EC2. It functions as both a disease identifier and cultivation advisor for rice farmers in Sri Lanka. By leveraging artificial intelligence, Verdex provides farmers with instant, accurate, and actionable cultivation plans directly on their smartphones, helping to bridge the critical information gap that limits productivity and profitability for approximately 1 million smallholder rice farmers.

The Problem

Sri Lankan agriculture, particularly the smallholder farming sector (approximately 1.65 million farmers), is significantly affected by crop losses due to pests and diseases, accounting for an estimated 10% loss in total yield.

üìâ Significant Yield Loss

Rice farmers lose 479 kg/acre (11%) in Yala season and 513 kg/acre (16%) in Maha season to pests and diseases. In severe outbreaks, losses can surge to over 30%.

üîç Knowledge Gap

Only ~36% of farmers can correctly self-identify common rice diseases. A mere 25-30% have regular access to expert advice from agricultural officers.

Current Challenges

  • Delayed Diagnosis: Agricultural officers may take days or weeks to visit, allowing disease to spread
  • Inaccurate Information: Farmers rely on guesswork or unreliable anecdotal advice
  • Resource Inefficiency: "Spray and pray" approach wastes money and harms the environment
  • Lack of Data: No real-time network for monitoring crop health across the country

Data Preprocessing

  • Image Standardization: All images resized to 224√ó224 pixels
  • Normalization: Pixel values scaled from [0, 255] to [0, 1]
  • Train/Validation Split: 80% training, 20% validation
  • Data Augmentation: Rotation, flipping, zooming, brightness adjustment to expand dataset

CNN Model Architecture

We use MobileNetV2 with Transfer Learning for efficient, high-accuracy inference.

Why MobileNetV2?

Depthwise Separable Convolutions dramatically reduce computational cost while maintaining accuracy. Pre-trained on ImageNet (14M+ images), enabling transfer learning with our smaller dataset. Optimized for mobile deployment with TensorFlow Lite conversion.

Transfer Learning Strategy

  1. Pre-trained Base: Start with MobileNetV2 pre-trained on ImageNet
  2. Feature Extraction: Freeze early convolutional layers to preserve learned features
  3. Custom Classifier: Replace original 1000-class head with 4-class output for our diseases
  4. Fine-tuning: Train only the new classifier head (optionally unfreeze last few layers)

Regularization Techniques

  • Data Augmentation: Real-time random transformations (rotation, flip, zoom, brightness)
  • Early Stopping: Monitor val_loss with patience=10 epochs to prevent overfitting
  • Model Checkpointing: Save best model when validation accuracy improves

Continuous Learning Loop

The training pipeline is designed for continuous improvement:

1 Ingest new data from experts & users
2 Train on AWS EC2 GPU instances
3 Validate on held-out test set
4 Version and store in model registry
5 Deploy if outperforms current model

Transparency & Limitations

The model displays a confidence score with every prediction. Low confidence signals uncertainty and recommends consulting a human expert. Known limitations include dependency on image quality, difficulty with early-stage symptoms, and inability to identify diseases not in the training set.
